Who Are We Really?

This podcast explores how personality tests like Myers-Briggs Type Indicator(MBTI) shape the way college students understand themselves and others. Through conversation, research, and real-life examples, we discuss why MBTI feels meaningful to so many people and why some psychologists question its accuracy. By looking at both the benefits and limitations of personality labels, this podcast invites listeners to reflect on identity, self-understanding, and the complexity of human personality.

Episode: Why MBTI Shouldn't Define You

Episode Notes

In this episode, we explore the popularity of the Myers-Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI) and discuss both why people find it meaningful and why psychologists often question its scientific accuracy. While MBTI can offer helpful insights into personal preferences and communication styles, relying too heavily on personality labels may limit how people see their potential.

We also discuss real-life examples where MBTI labels influence career choices, relationships, and personal confidence. Research in psychology suggests that personality traits exist on a spectrum and that skills such as leadership, communication, and confidence can develop over time. Because of this, many psychologists recommend using personality frameworks as tools for reflection rather than fixed definitions of identity.

Ultimately, MBTI can be fun and insightful, but it should not define who we are or what we are capable of becoming.
