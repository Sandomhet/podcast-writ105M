Campus Voices

Campus Voices is a student-run podcast from the university, exploring ideas, research, and stories from our community. Each episode features conversations with faculty, students, and guests on topics that matter. Tune in for thoughtful discussions that go beyond the lecture hall.

Episode 1: Why We Study — Finding Your Path in Academia

Summary

In this first episode we talk about how different students and researchers found their way into their fields, the role of curiosity and mentorship, and practical advice for anyone still figuring out what they want to study. Our guests share personal stories and lessons learned along the way.

Sources & further reading

  • University Office of Undergraduate Research — Getting Started Guide
  • “The Value of a Liberal Education” — Faculty op-ed, Campus Daily
  • Study on student motivation and persistence (2022) — Research & Assessment

Full episode script

Host

Welcome to Campus Voices. Today we’re asking one big question: why do we study what we study? I’m joined by two guests who’ll share how they found their path.

Guest 1

For me it started with one class in my second year. I didn’t expect to like it as much as I did, but the professor kept opening doors to new questions. That’s when I knew I wanted to go deeper.

Host

And you, how did you end up in your current field?

Guest 2

I actually switched majors. It felt like a big risk at the time, but talking to advisors and other students helped. The key was giving myself permission to change direction when something felt like a better fit.

Host

Both of you mention the importance of people — professors, advisors, peers. What would you tell someone who’s still unsure?

Guest 1

Try things. Take a class outside your comfort zone, go to office hours, join a reading group. You don’t have to have it all figured out. Curiosity is enough to start.

Guest 2

And it’s okay to change your mind. Your path doesn’t have to be a straight line. Thanks for having us.

Host

Thank you both. That’s it for this episode. If you liked what you heard, share it with a friend. We’ll be back soon with another conversation. See you then.
